BERKELEY DIVISION OF DATA SCIENCES

SUMMARY

Teaching is one of my greatest passions. It combines my interests in cognitive psychology, communication, and social change. The Berkeley CS department's opportunities for undergraduate students to serve on the course staff allows me to also incorporate my interest in computer science into teaching.

I currently serve on the Fall 2018 cohort of CS 370 (Introduction to Teaching Computer Science), a selective course that teaches aspiring CS course instructor how to effectively educate students. The cohort focuses on the concepts behind teaching individuals vs. groups and the psycho-social factors behind learning.

As a member of the CS 370 cohort, I also hold weekly tutoring sessions for students in Berkeley's lower division computer science and data science classes.

I am concurrently serving as an academic intern for Berkeley's introductory data science course Foundations of Data Science, where I assist the undergraduate student instructors in providing academic support in lab sessions.
